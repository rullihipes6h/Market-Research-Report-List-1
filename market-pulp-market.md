<p><p>The market pulp is categorized into three main types: Bleached Softwood Kraft (BSK), Bleached Hardwood Kraft (BHK), and High-Yield Pulp (HYP). BSK, derived from softwood, is primarily used in high-quality paper products due to its strength and durability. BHK is sourced from hardwood, offering improved brightness and smoothness for commercial papers. HYP, made from non-wood fibers or low-quality pulps, focuses on delivering high yield, often used in newsprint and lower-grade papers. Each type serves distinct market needs.</p></p>
