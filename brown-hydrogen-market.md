<p><p>Brown hydrogen is produced primarily through coal gasification, a process that converts coal into hydrogen and other byproducts. The market can be divided into two types: coal gasification with carbon capture storage (CCS) and coal gasification without CCS. The former focuses on minimizing environmental impact by capturing and storing carbon emissions, appealing to sustainability-driven industries. Conversely, coal gasification without CCS prioritizes cost-effectiveness, often resulting in higher emissions, primarily targeted at markets unconcerned with carbon footprint regulations.</p></p>
