<p><p>The smartphone battery market primarily features Lithium-Ion (Li-ion) and Nickel Metal Hydride (NiMH) batteries. Li-ion batteries dominate due to their higher energy density, lighter weight, and longer lifespan, making them ideal for modern smartphones requiring extended usage and faster charging. In contrast, NiMH batteries, though less commonly used, offer advantages like better environmental safety and lower cost but have a shorter lifespan and lower energy capacity. Both types cater to varying consumer needs and environmental considerations in the smartphone industry.</p></p>
