<p><p>Waste food management in commercial kitchens focuses on efficiently processing organic waste to minimize environmental impact. Aerobic digestion involves microorganisms breaking down organic material in the presence of oxygen, resulting in compost and biogas. This method is effective for high-moisture, easily degradable waste. In contrast, anaerobic digestion occurs without oxygen, using bacteria to decompose organic matter, producing biogas and digestate. This process is ideal for large-scale operations, turning food scraps into renewable energy while reducing landfill disposal.</p></p>
