<p><p>The Fluid-applied Waterproofing Membrane market is divided into two primary types: Cold Fluid-applied and Hot Fluid-applied membranes. Cold Fluid-applied membranes are typically used in moderate temperature environments, offering ease of application and quick curing times. In contrast, Hot Fluid-applied membranes are designed for high-temperature applications, providing enhanced durability and waterproofing effectiveness. Both types are widely utilized in construction and infrastructure to protect structures from water infiltration and extend their lifespan, catering to varied environmental and project-specific needs.</p></p>
